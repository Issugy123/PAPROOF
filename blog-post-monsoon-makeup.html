Monsoon Season Makeup: Tips for Humidity-Proof Looks

The Philippine monsoon season brings refreshing rain but also presents unique challenges for makeup wearers. High humidity, unexpected downpours, and the constant transition between muggy outdoors and air-conditioned indoors can wreak havoc on even the most carefully applied makeup. But don't worry – with the right techniques and products, you can achieve a beautiful, long-lasting look that withstands the rainy season.

In this guide, we'll share expert tips and product recommendations to help you create humidity-proof makeup looks that stay fresh from morning to night, no matter what the monsoon weather brings.

Prep Your Canvas: Skincare is Key

The foundation of humidity-proof makeup starts with proper skincare. During monsoon season, your skin's needs change:

A lightweight, oil-controlling skincare routine is essential during monsoon season

  1. Switch to a gel-based cleanser - Our Bubble Fresh Gel Cleanser removes excess oil without stripping the skin.
  2. Use a lightweight, oil-free moisturizer - Heavy creams can feel suffocating in humidity. Try our Bubble Dew Water-Gel Moisturizer for hydration without heaviness.

  5. Consider a setting spray before foundation - This might sound counterintuitive, but a light mist of setting spray before applying foundation can create an additional barrier against humidity.

Foundation: Go Light and Waterproof

Heavy foundation is the first to melt in humidity. Here's how to keep your base looking fresh:

  1. Choose waterproof or water-resistant formulas - Our Bubble Stay All Day Foundation is designed to withstand humidity and light rain.
  2. Consider a tinted moisturizer or BB cream - Lighter coverage products like our Bubble Tint SPF 50 Tinted Moisturizer are less likely to slide off in humidity.
  3. Apply with a damp makeup sponge - This helps the product meld with your skin rather than sitting on top where it can easily slide off.
  4. Use less product - A thin layer is less likely to move around than a thick application. Focus on areas that need coverage and leave the rest of your skin bare.
  5. Set immediately - Don't wait to set your foundation. Apply powder while the foundation is still slightly tacky for maximum adherence.

Powder: Your Humidity-Fighting Hero

Strategic powdering is crucial for monsoon makeup longevity:

  1. Use translucent setting powder - Our Bubble Set Translucent Powder absorbs excess oil without adding heaviness or color.
  2. Focus on the T-zone - Apply powder generously to areas that get oily first: forehead, nose, and chin.
  3. Press, don't sweep - Use a pressing motion with your powder puff or sponge to set makeup rather than sweeping, which can disturb your foundation.
  4. Carry blotting papers - Our Bubble Blot Oil-Absorbing Papers remove excess oil throughout the day without disturbing makeup.

Pro tip: The "baking" technique works wonders during monsoon season. Apply a generous amount of translucent powder to areas prone to creasing or oiliness, let it sit for 5-10 minutes while your body heat helps it set the makeup, then dust away the excess.

Eyes: Waterproof Everything

Nothing ruins a makeup look faster than raccoon eyes from smudged mascara or eyeliner:

  1. Prime your eyelids - Our Bubble Eye Primer creates a smooth, oil-resistant base for eyeshadow.
  2. Choose cream or powder eyeshadows - Avoid cream-to-powder formulas, which can crease in humidity. Our Bubble Eyes powder shadows are formulated to resist creasing even in high humidity.
  3. Set cream products with powder - If you use cream eyeshadow, always set it with a matching powder shade.
  4. Use waterproof eyeliner - Our Bubble Stay-Put Waterproof Eyeliner won't budge, even in a downpour.
  5. Waterproof mascara is non-negotiable - Our Bubble Lash Waterproof Mascara stays put through rain, sweat, and tears.
  6. Consider skipping lower lash mascara - This is often the first to smudge in humidity.

Cheeks: Layering for Longevity

Blush and bronzer can disappear quickly in humid weather. Here's how to make them last:

  1. Layer cream and powder products - Apply a cream blush or bronzer first, then set with a matching powder product for double the staying power.
  2. Choose water-resistant formulas - Our Bubble Stain Cheek Tints are designed to resist moisture and provide long-lasting color.
  3. Apply after setting your foundation - This prevents the colors from blending too much with your base.
  4. Go lighter than usual - Humidity can intensify blush and bronzer as the day goes on, so start with a lighter application than you normally would.

Lips: Long-lasting Color

Lip color can fade quickly in humid weather, especially if you're constantly drinking water (as you should be!):

  1. Start with a lip liner - Line and fill in your entire lip with our Bubble Lip Liner to create a base that helps color last longer.
  2. Choose long-wear formulas - Our Bubble Stay Matte Liquid Lipsticks are designed to last through eating, drinking, and humidity.
  3. Consider lip stains - Our Bubble Tint Lip Stains leave a long-lasting wash of color that won't budge.
  4. Blot and set - After applying lipstick, blot with a tissue, apply a second layer, then lightly dust with translucent powder for maximum longevity.
  5. Carry your lip color for touch-ups - Even the most long-lasting formulas may need a refresh after eating.

Setting: The Final Shield

The right setting techniques can make or break your monsoon makeup look:

  1. Use a waterproof setting spray - Our Bubble Lock It Setting Spray creates a water-resistant shield over your makeup.
  2. Apply in layers - For maximum protection, apply setting spray after foundation, again after powder products, and a final layer when your makeup is complete.
  3. Allow each layer to dry completely - Don't rush the process; let each layer set before moving on.
  4. Hold the bottle at least 8 inches from your face - This ensures an even, fine mist rather than droplets that could disturb your makeup.

Touch-up Kit: Monsoon Edition

Be prepared for touch-ups with a well-stocked makeup bag:

  • Blotting papers (our Bubble Blot sheets are perfect)
  • Mini translucent powder compact with puff
  • Q-tips for fixing smudged eye makeup
  • Your lip color of the day
  • Mini setting spray
  • Waterproof concealer for spot touch-ups

Monsoon Makeup Looks to Try

Some makeup styles work better than others during the rainy season:

1. The Minimalist

Less is more during monsoon season. Try:

  • Waterproof tinted moisturizer
  • Cream blush set with powder blush
  • Waterproof mascara
  • Tinted lip balm

2. The Monochromatic

Using the same color family for eyes, cheeks, and lips simplifies touch-ups:

  • Waterproof foundation
  • Bronze eyeshadow
  • Bronzer on cheeks
  • Bronze-toned lip color

3. The Stained

Stains and tints last longer than traditional formulas:

  • Waterproof base
  • Cheek stain
  • Clear brow gel
  • Waterproof mascara
  • Lip stain

Final Tips

Remember these additional monsoon makeup hacks:

  • Carry a small umbrella - The best way to protect your makeup is to avoid getting it wet in the first place!
  • Waterproof your mascara - If you don't have waterproof mascara, apply a clear waterproof topcoat over your regular mascara.
  • Avoid touching your face - This transfers oils from your fingers to your makeup, breaking it down faster.
  • Skip the highlighter - Or use it very sparingly, as humidity already creates a natural sheen.
  • Embrace the dewy look - Sometimes it's better to work with the humidity rather than fighting it. A strategically dewy finish can look intentional rather than like your makeup is melting.

With these tips and the right products, you can achieve beautiful, long-lasting makeup looks even during the most humid monsoon days. Remember that practice makes perfect – you might need to experiment to find the exact combination of products and techniques that work best for your skin type and local climate conditions.
